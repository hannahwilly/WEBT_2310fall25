Local Fall Events

Discover the Best Fall Activities in Your Area

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, it's the perfect time to explore the great outdoors and enjoy seasonal festivities. Whether you're looking for scenic hikes, pumpkin patches, or local events, we've got you covered! Below I have listed some local favorites but this is just a small sampling of the many places to visit in the area. For more ideas visit hopemaum.com

Flatop Mountain Farm

Nestled in the rolling hills of Soddy-Daisy, Tennessee, Flat Top Mountain Farm is a multi-generational, family-owned working farm that offers seasonal charm and scenic escapes all year long. Located at 638 Bowman Cemetery Road, the farm invites visitors to immerse themselves in nature through experiences like flower fields, pumpkin patches, and a twirling corn maze. In summer, guests can stroll through blooming beds of zinnias and sunflowers; during its famed Lavender Festival, the farm features self pick lavender bundles, artisan goods, and live bluegrass music. Come fall, the farm transforms into a seasonal playground with hayrides offering sweeping mountain views, sprawling pumpkin assortments, and maze adventures that pair perfectly with crisp air and colorful landscapes. Beyond the fun, it’s a space to slow down, connect with nature, and appreciate the heritage of Tennessee agriculture—a memorable destination whether you’re picking blooms, navigating the maze, or simply enjoying the view.

Hamilton County Fair

Every autumn, McDonald Farm in Sale Creek becomes the vibrant setting for the Little Debbie Hamilton County Fair, a beloved three-day event taking place over Veterans Day weekend. With its mix of classic fair delights—amusement rides, pig races, lumberjack shows, and a rodeo—alongside live concert performances from national acts like Diamond Rio, the fair captures something for all ages. More than just entertainment, the fair celebrates local agriculture and the rich heritage of the region, offering agriculture exhibits, blue-ribbon competitions, and a nod to community values and tradition. With free parking, special discounts for veterans, and an overall family-friendly atmosphere, the fair is an inviting way for residents and visitors alike to come together, enjoy fall festivities, and make lasting memories.

Dread Hollow

Located at 321 Browns Ferry Road in Chattanooga, Tennessee, Dread Hollow is a top-tier haunted attraction with immersive experiences built to thrill. Every autumn (from early October through early November) the venue transforms into a fully detailed horror setting featuring three main nightmares: the decaying “Belle Royale Hotel,” the eerie “Dreadwood Forest,” and the sinister halls of “Dread Hollow High School.” The Scare Factor rates Dread Hollow as one of the most intense haunted houses in the country, thanks to its high-quality set designs, professional actors, and innovative scare tactics. Visitors also have the option to take on escape-rooms themed around serial killers and abandoned institutions, and there is a special “R.I.P.” ticket upgrade that grants access to a lounge where you can participate in triggering scares. With timed‐entry tickets, strong attention to set design and actor performance, and a reputation for being one of Chattanooga’s top haunted house events, Dread Hollow offers a standout experience for thrill-seekers.
